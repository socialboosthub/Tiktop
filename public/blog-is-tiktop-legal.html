Understanding Copyright

Creators own the copyright to the videos they create. Downloading a video for personal offline viewing is different from claiming ownership of that content.

If you plan to reuse someone else's video, you should always respect copyright laws, the creator's rights, and the applicable laws in your country.

What Is Fair Use?

In some countries, copyright laws allow limited use of copyrighted material under principles commonly known as fair use or similar legal exceptions. Examples may include:

  • Educational purposes
  • News reporting
  • Commentary
  • Criticism
  • Reviews
  • Research

Whether a particular use qualifies depends on the laws where you live and the specific circumstances.

Using TikTop Responsibly

TikTop encourages users to download content responsibly. Good practices include:

  • Download only public videos.
  • Respect original creators.
  • Credit creators whenever appropriate.
  • Do not impersonate creators.
  • Do not remove ownership or branding to mislead others.
  • Do not upload another person's content as your own.

When Downloading Can Become a Problem

While downloading itself may be permitted in many situations, problems can arise if downloaded content is misused.

  • Re-uploading copyrighted videos without permission.
  • Selling another creator's work.
  • Pretending you created the content.
  • Using copyrighted material in ways that violate applicable law.
  • Harassing or misleading others using downloaded videos.
